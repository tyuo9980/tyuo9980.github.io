Bubblescape

Java
Android
Github Repo
Google Play

Bubblescape is an original game made by RICE, a group of aspiring high school students that had nothing better to do over the summer. You play as an invisible entity that helps Miles the duck reach the surface as he's trapped in a bubble deep under the ocean.

Our game was based off the idea of an unique control system. Many other games simply use an accellerometer, simple directional swipes, and taps. However, Bubblescape uses the idea of swipes as currents that push objects. The biggest challenge was to accomodate the many resolutions and pixel densities of Android devices. To fix this problem, all images were created to fit the most common largest screen size and rendered at that resolution, then everything was scaled appropriately.

I was the lead developer and manager of the project. I assigned tasks to each member, set deadlines, and organized weekly meetings to make sure things got done on time. Although it wasn't professional, I experienced and learned a lot about the development cycle.
